Sweet and Sour Pork

Recipe Details:
Level of difficulties
Serving 4 pax
Cooking duration 25 minutes
Nutritional Facts:
Calories 515 kJ

Ingredients

    For the Pork:

  1. 1 ½ pounds boneless pork loin or boneless pork shoulder (diced into ½-inch cubes)
  2. 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  3. ¼ cup finely ground rice flour
  4. ¼ cup vegetable oil of choice (divided)
  5. 1 bell pepper (colour of choice, deseeded and diced into ½-inch cubes)
  6. 1 red onion (diced into ½-inch cubes)
  7. 2 large tomatoes (deseeded and diced into ½-inch cubes)
  8. 3 cloves garlic (finely chopped or grated)

    9. For the Sweet and Sour Sauce:

  9. ⅓ cup packed brown sugar (dark brown sugar, if available)
  10. ¼ cup rice wine vinegar
  11. 3 tablespoons ketchup
  12. 1–2 tablespoons soy sauce (sub tamari if gluten-free)

Method

  1. Season the cubed boneless pork loin (or shoulder) with kosher salt and lightly sprinkle the rice flour over top, tossing gently to coat. Set aside.
  2. Add all the ingredients for the homemade sweet and sour sauce into a bowl and whisk to combine.
  3. Heat up a little bit of oil in a skillet. Once it is hot, toss in all the veggies and quickly cook until tender-crisp. Set aside.
  4. Add some fresh oil to the same skillet you used to cook the veggies, then carefully toss in the lightly dredged pork. Cook the pork just until nicely browned, about 90 seconds per side.
  5. Pour the sweet and sour sauce into the skillet with the pork and cook for a couple of minutes.
  6. Once the sauce has coated the pork, add the veggies back in, tossing to combine. Serve hot.
