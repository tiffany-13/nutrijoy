About NutriJoy

Vision:

To inspire and empower people worldwide to embrace a healthy lifestyle by making nutritious, authentic Chinese cuisine accessible and enjoyable for everyone, regardless of their cooking experience.

Mission:

NutriJoy’s mission is to share easy-to-cook, health-conscious Chinese recipes that balance traditional flavors with modern dietary needs. I aim to promote home-cooked meals, guide individuals toward healthier eating habits, and provide resources for quick and delicious Chinese dishes that cater to various dietary preferences. Through my expertise in culinary arts and dietetics, we strive to make healthy eating simple, flavorful, and culturally enriching.

About Chinese Culinary

Introduction

Chinese cuisine is one of the oldest and most diverse in the world. It is characterized by its use of a wide variety of ingredients, including meats, seafood, vegetables, and grains. Chinese cooks also use a wide range of techniques, such stir-frying, steaming, and braising, to bring out the flavors of these ingredients.

History

During the Han Dynasty (206 BC-220 AD), the earliest Chinese cookbook was written. During the Shang Dynasty (16th-11th century BC), Chinese cuisine began to take shape.Over the centuries, dynasties developed unique styles of cooking. Imperial court during the Ming Dynasty (1368-1644) was known for luxurious dishes.

Healthy Diet Tips

Eat healthy. Stay healthy.

1 / 4

Eat a high-protein breakfast

While you may think that skipping meals will help with weight loss, not eating a nutritious breakfast can make it difficult to concentrate and leave you feeling hungry, irritable, and less productive. And, you’re more likely to be so hungry by lunch that you will overeat.

2 / 4

Blend up a green smoothie

A smoothie is also a great post-workout snack. Experiment with different ingredients, including fresh and frozen fruits and veggies, nuts and seeds, Greek yogurt, and protein powders.

3 / 4

Stay hydrated and watch what you drink

Busy individuals often rely on coffee for energy, but excessive caffeine can cause harm. Instead of sugary sodas or juices that contain added sugars, health experts recommend plain water to stay hydrated.

4 / 4

Pack your lunch

Fast food may be convenient, but it’s often filled with calories, unhealthy fats, and sodium. Rather than grabbing fast food for lunch, pack your lunch the night before so you can control what you eat and avoid unhealthy options.
